Welcome to Congaree National Park

Congaree National Park protects the largest remnant of old-growth floodplain forest in North America, preserving an ecosystem that has all but disappeared from the southern United States.

A Wilderness Like No Other

Designated as a National Park in 2003, Congaree's 26,000 acres of pristine wilderness contain some of the tallest trees in eastern North America, with champion specimens reaching record heights.

  • Loblolly pines over 170 feet tall
  • Ancient bald cypress trees
  • Diverse sweetgum and oak species

A Unique Floodplain Ecosystem

The park's floodplain forest experiences regular flooding from the Congaree and Wateree Rivers, creating a rich habitat for diverse plant and animal life.
